<br/>*Motivation<br/>To be able to manage their care process, patients/customers need insight into the quality of care provided. Only then can they base their choices on the right information. But the registration burden in healthcare is high and the supply of (extra) quality information increases this burden even more. Data to be recorded must have added value for the (care for) the patient or contribute to determining the (quality of) care provided.<br/><br/>*Rationale<br/>By realizing the desired transparency with unambiguous, anonymous data from the primary source registration, (satisfaction) measurements, and information to be derived from this, the registration burden can be limited. This also applies to transparency towards the government, insurers, or other organizations involved in healthcare.<br/><br/>*Implications<br/>·     (Re)design of processes based on value for the patient (outcomes, outcome)<br/>·     Quality registrations and other deliveries entail as little extra work as possible; this means:<br/>—  The quality records are based on information from the primary process<br/>—  Different quality registrations request similar information in the same way<br/>—  Record data in accordance with the healthcare information building blocks<br/>·     Data capturing details of the patient experience is also required for complete transparency; this means:<br/>—  Structural satisfaction and outcome measurements of the patient as part of the process<br/>—  Drawing up and adjusting questionnaires in consultation with the patient, so that attention is paid to what they find important<br/>·     The technical design of systems should ensure, to the extent possible, that administrative burden is kept to a minimum; this means:<br/>—  Data is recorded unambiguously to enable multiple use (see also Basic Principle 3)<br/>—  For optimum user-friendliness, interfaces are designed in close collaboration with end users; e.g., using principles from interaction design and user experience design<br/>·     Healthcare providers record information clearly and once; this means:<br/>—  Do not record something that has already been recorded<br/>—  Providers should be aware of the usefulness and necessity of this<br/>—  Structural support for caregivers in the use of quality improvement systems<br/>—  Use semantic standards and the healthcare information building blocks<br/>
