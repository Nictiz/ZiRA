Medical restraints are (physical or verbal) measures used to restrict the freedom of the patient in question. <br/>In hospitals, these often include the use of side rails, a cushion belt (around the waist), limb restraints or a Posey bed. Medical restraints are often used in combination with sedatives. <br/>Medical restraints may only be implemented under strict conditions. The Vereniging Verpleegkundigen & Verzorgenden Nederland (V&VN) [Dutch Nurses and Carers Association] published a set of guidelines for nurses to implement these restraints in hospitals. <br/>The patient's high-risk behavior providing cause for the restraints is not described in this concept.<br/>
